# Market Regime Playbook

## Market Regime Identification

### Bull Market Regime
**Characteristics:**
- Price above 200-day moving average
- RSI consistently above 50
- Higher highs and higher lows
- Volume increasing on up days
- Low volatility (VIX < 20)

**Trading Strategy:**
- Focus on trend following strategies
- Use wider stop losses (2-3%)
- Pyramid winning positions
- Reduce position sizing on pullbacks

**Risk Management:**
- Maximum 5% portfolio risk
- Trail stops on winners
- Avoid short positions
- Maintain cash for opportunities

### Bear Market Regime
**Characteristics:**
- Price below 200-day moving average
- RSI consistently below 50
- Lower highs and lower lows
- Volume increasing on down days
- High volatility (VIX > 30)

**Trading Strategy:**
- Focus on mean reversion strategies
- Use tighter stop losses (1-2%)
- Short positions with tight risk
- Cash preservation priority

**Risk Management:**
- Maximum 2% portfolio risk
- Quick stop losses
- Avoid long positions
- High cash allocation (60%+)

### Sideways/Choppy Market Regime
**Characteristics:**
- Price trading around key moving averages
- RSI oscillating around 50
- No clear trend direction
- Mixed volume patterns
- Moderate volatility

**Trading Strategy:**
- Range-bound strategies
- Mean reversion plays
- Tight risk management
- Reduced position sizes

**Risk Management:**
- Maximum 3% portfolio risk
- Tight stop losses (1%)
- Both long and short positions
- Avoid trend following strategies

### Volatility Regime
**High Volatility (VIX > 30):**
- Reduce position sizes by 50%
- Wider stop losses
- Focus on options strategies
- Increased cash reserves

**Low Volatility (VIX < 15):**
- Normal position sizes
- Tighter stop losses
- Trend following strategies
- Full position allocation

## Regime Change Indicators

### Bull to Bear Transition
- Death cross (50-day MA below 200-day MA)
- RSI breaks below 40 support
- Increasing volume on down days
- VIX spike above 30
- Break of key support levels

### Bear to Bull Transition
- Golden cross (50-day MA above 200-day MA)
- RSI breaks above 60 resistance
- Increasing volume on up days
- VIX decline below 20
- Break of key resistance levels

### Sideways to Trend Transition
- Break of range boundaries
- Sustained move above/below key MAs
- Volume confirmation
- Momentum shift
- Volatility expansion

## Regime-Specific Rules

### Bull Market Rules
- Buy dips above 50-day MA
- Sell rallies below 200-day MA
- Use 2:1 risk/reward minimum
- Hold winners 3-5 days minimum
- Scale in gradually

### Bear Market Rules
- Short rallies below 50-day MA
- Cover shorts near support
- Use 1.5:1 risk/reward minimum
- Quick profit taking (1-2 days)
- Scale out quickly

### Sideways Market Rules
- Buy at range bottom, sell at top
- Use range boundaries for stops
- Use 1:1 risk/reward minimum
- Quick entries and exits
- No overnight positions

## Risk Adjustments by Regime

### Position Sizing
- **Bull Market**: 100% of normal size
- **Bear Market**: 50% of normal size
- **Sideways Market**: 75% of normal size
- **Transition Periods**: 25% of normal size

### Stop Loss Distance
- **Bull Market**: 2-3%
- **Bear Market**: 1-2%
- **Sideways Market**: 1-1.5%
- **High Volatility**: Add 1% to normal

### Portfolio Allocation
- **Bull Market**: 80% equities, 20% cash
- **Bear Market**: 20% equities, 80% cash
- **Sideways Market**: 50% equities, 50% cash
- **Transition**: 40% equities, 60% cash
